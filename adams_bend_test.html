Adam's Bend Test Guide

A simple guide to screen for potential signs of scoliosis at home.

What is the Adam's Bend Test?

The Adam's forward bend test is a simple screening method used to check for asymmetry in the spine, which can be a sign of scoliosis. It involves bending forward at the waist and observing the back for any unevenness.

Important Disclaimer:

This is not a diagnostic tool. It is a simple observation guide. Only a medical professional can diagnose scoliosis, typically with an X-ray. Please consult a doctor if you have any concerns.

How to Perform the Test

1

Stand Up Straight

Have the person being screened stand with their feet together, knees straight, and arms hanging freely at their sides.

2

Bend Forward Slowly

Ask them to bend forward slowly, as if diving into a pool, with their palms together and arms pointing towards the floor. Their head should be down, and they should stop when their back is parallel to the floor.

3

Observe from Behind

Stand behind the person and look along their back from one end to the other. You are looking for any asymmetry (unevenness).

What to Look For

When looking at the back, check for any of the following signs:

  • One shoulder blade is higher or more prominent than the other.
  • One side of the rib cage appears higher than the other (a "rib hump").
  • The lower back appears uneven.
  • The hips appear to be at different heights.
  • The space between the arms and the body is different from one side to the other.

Next Steps

If you notice any asymmetry, it is recommended to schedule an appointment with a pediatrician or an orthopedic specialist. They can perform a more thorough examination and, if necessary, order an X-ray to confirm a diagnosis and determine the severity (Cobb angle) of the curve.
